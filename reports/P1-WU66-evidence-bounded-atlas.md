# P1-WU66 Evidence-bounded primary-branch atlas

Date: 2026-07-12

Registered experiment: `P1-E006-evidence-bounded-atlas`

## Production scope

The experiment generated 1,446 process-distance-parameter records:

- 160 finite-disk M2 tide transfer records;
- one registered Helgoland BSH storm-surge direct-attraction record;
- five catalogue-mean Gaussian SSH eddy records;
- 640 exactly compensated internal-wave dipole records;
- 480 exactly mass-balanced tsunami packet records; and
- 160 Storegga named-event mass-conserving slide records.

All varied parameters retain the manifest label
`sensitivity_design_not_probability`. No record is interpreted as a percentile
or occurrence probability.

## Geometry correction gate

The preregistered first attempt used near-plane polar midpoint integration for
Gaussian surface loads. Its tsunami near field violated the continuous-sheet
physical amplitude bound and was rejected before registration. The corrected
experiment places compact surface anomalies on a spherical Earth and measures
great-circle standoff from their represented three-sigma support. The anomalies
translate parallel to an idealized coastline, so no source cell coincides with
the surface station. Tsunami crest and trough masses are normalized to cancel
at every time step.

Corrected tsunami peak direct gravity decreases from approximately
`3.15e-10--6.49e-10 m s^-2` at 10 km standoff to
`4.93e-12--1.27e-11 m s^-2` at 1,000 km. The rejected near-plane artifact is not
retained as a scientific result.

## Frequency-coverage result

None of the 1,446 records meets the predeclared 90% signal-energy coverage gate
for all three admitted vertical-gravity literature anchors:

- M2 tide, storm surge, mesoscale eddy and internal wave have no covered
  spectral interval;
- tsunami and Storegga records have partial coverage but remain below the 90%
  threshold.

Consequently the production matrix contains no decision-grade detectable or
undetectable classification. This is a coverage-limited negative result, not a
claim that the physical signals are absent. The instrument curves are not
extrapolated below their published/declared frequency support.
