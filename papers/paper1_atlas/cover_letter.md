# Cover letter draft — Earth and Space Science

Dear Editor,

Please consider the technical preprint, “Frequency-Coverage Limits in Assessing
Gravity Signals from Oceanic Mass Redistribution.” The study develops a common,
evidence-bounded direct-gravity framework for six representative ocean-process
families and asks whether published instrument-noise evidence covers enough
signal energy to authorize an SNR-based classification.

The principal result is deliberately negative but actionable: none of 1,446
registered scenario records has sufficient spectral-energy coverage under the
three reviewed vertical-gravity literature anchors. Instead of extrapolating
short-term sensitivity curves, the paper reports process-specific lower-band
requirements. A registered review audit confirms the result using the
primary-source AQG/FG5 white-noise interval and alternative finite-record energy
conventions. The software reproduces 50 published LoadDef benchmark rows and a
separate open Helgoland model comparison within a preregistered tolerance.

The manuscript does not claim a completed detectability atlas, universal
non-detectability, or total terrestrial observables for every process. Code,
registered compact metrics, tests, figures and the manuscript are released
under version `paper1-v1.0.0`; restricted and bulk inputs are represented by
manifests rather than redistributed.

The work is not under journal consideration in this repository-release form.
Before journal submission, the contributors will replace collective project
authorship with the approved named author order, affiliations, corresponding
author, acknowledgements, funding and conflict declarations.

Sincerely,

Ocean Gravity Program contributors
